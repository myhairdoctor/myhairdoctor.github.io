Explore Effective Treatments

Hair loss can often be managed with the right treatments. Below, we delve into proven options, their efficacy, and considerations for each.

Medications

Medications such as Finasteride, Dutasteride, and Minoxidil are commonly prescribed for hair loss. Here’s what the research says:

Finasteride vs. Dutasteride

  • Finasteride: Shown to reduce hair loss in 65% of men over 2 years with minimal side effects like decreased libido (around 1.8% of users).
  • Dutasteride: More effective than Finasteride, with the Indian Journal of Dermatology reporting better hair density outcomes. However, it is not FDA-approved for hair loss in the USA but is widely used in Korea and Japan.

Minoxidil

Topical Minoxidil stimulates hair growth by improving blood flow to hair follicles. Clinical trials show:

  • Men: 40-60% experience visible improvement within 4-6 months.
  • Women: 60% report hair regrowth in areas of thinning.
  • Side Effects: Scalp irritation and shedding during the initial weeks of use.

PRP Therapy

Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy involves injecting your own plasma into the scalp to stimulate hair growth. According to the Indian Journal of Dermatology:

  • Men showed a statistically significant increase in hair density.
  • Women did not show statistically significant results.

PRP often requires 3-6 sessions for noticeable results and can be used as a complementary treatment to hair transplants.

Hair Transplants

Hair transplantation is a surgical solution for restoring hair in areas of significant thinning or baldness. Key facts include:

  • Methods: Follicular Unit Transplantation (FUT) and Follicular Unit Extraction (FUE).
  • Success Rate: 85-95% graft survival rate reported in clinical studies.
  • Considerations: Requires downtime, and results take 6-12 months to fully manifest.
