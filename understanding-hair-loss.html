What Causes Hair Loss?

Hair loss is a complex issue with various causes, ranging from genetic predisposition to lifestyle factors. Understanding the root cause is essential for effective treatment.

Causes of Hair Loss

  • Genetics: Androgenetic alopecia, or pattern hair loss, is inherited and affects both men and women.
  • Hormonal Imbalances: Conditions like PCOS and hormonal shifts during pregnancy or menopause can contribute to hair loss.
  • Medical Conditions: Thyroid disorders, alopecia areata, and certain autoimmune diseases can lead to hair loss.
  • Stress and Lifestyle: High stress levels, poor diet, and lack of sleep can exacerbate hair shedding.
  • Medications: Drugs for chemotherapy, blood pressure, and other conditions often list hair loss as a side effect.

The Science Behind Hair Loss

Hair loss occurs when the hair growth cycle is disrupted. This cycle consists of three main phases:

  1. Anagen: The active growth phase lasting 2-7 years.
  2. Catagen: The transition phase lasting about 10 days.
  3. Telogen: The resting phase, after which hair sheds and the cycle restarts.

Conditions like androgenetic alopecia shorten the anagen phase, causing hair follicles to shrink and produce thinner, weaker hair over time.

Diagnosing Hair Loss

Accurate diagnosis is crucial for effective treatment. Common diagnostic methods include:

  • Scalp Examination: A visual assessment of hair thinning and scalp health.
  • Blood Tests: To identify underlying conditions like thyroid imbalances or nutrient deficiencies.
  • Trichoscopy: A detailed examination of the scalp and hair follicles using a specialized device.
